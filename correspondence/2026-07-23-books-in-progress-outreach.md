**Subject:** Does Books in Progress take outside authors?

Hello,

I watched Stewart Brand's *Maintenance* serialize on Books in Progress, and it's shaped how I'm publishing my current project. Before I build the whole distribution stack myself, I wanted to ask: do you take books from outside authors, or is the platform reserved for Stripe Press titles?

The book is about AI evaluation evidence: what an eval result licenses someone to conclude, and what a deployer should demand before signing off. It's written for the two sides of one handoff, the people who produce assurance evidence and the people who carry the liability for acting on it. Every chapter opens with a documented case (the *Mata v. Avianca* sanctions, *Moffatt v. Air Canada*) verified against primary sources, and closes with what documentation would have made the failure diagnosable. It grows out of four research papers of mine on adversarial pragmatics and evaluation assurance.

It's being built in public now (github.com/BrettRey/language-mediated-control), releasing chapter by chapter at reviewable-draft quality, versioned, free to read.

If Books in Progress is open to outside work, I'd be glad to hear what you'd need to see. If it isn't, thanks anyway, and thanks for the Brand serialization either way: it made the case that this model works.
